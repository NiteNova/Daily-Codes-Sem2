Basic Facts about Jerry Lawson


- Born on December 1, 1940 in New York Cty
- Died on April 9, 2011 in Santa Clara, California
- Grew up learning how to repair television sets
- He attended both Queens College and City College of New York, but did not complete a degree at either.

Who was Jerry Lawson?


Jerry Lawson was an hardware engineer for Fairchild's video game division. He helped with the development of pong.
He was the led developer of the Fairchild Cahnnel F console. His invention was the swappable game cartridges.
His legacy still lives on as he was one of the pioneers of gaming as his invention with the game cartridges
help propel other products like the Atari 2600.

What else did Jerry Lawson have in store during his career?

He created an arcade game called Demolition Derby in this garage. Lawson also created
the prototype for the first digital joystick for the home console. He had met Steve Jobs
and Steve Wozniak when he was part of the HomeBrew Computer Club.
